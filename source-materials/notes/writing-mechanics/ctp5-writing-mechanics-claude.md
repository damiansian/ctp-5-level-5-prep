### Questions 1–8: Punctuation Errors

**Directions:** For questions 1–8, find the punctuation error, if there is one, in the sentence. Some sentences will not have any punctuation errors. If there is no punctuation error in the sentence, circle in choice D on your answer sheet.

**1.** Margret who is always late for class, was given detention for a week. No error
- A. "Margret who" (underlined)
- B. "class," (underlined)
- C. "week." (underlined)
- D. No error

> Student answer: A ✓
> Note: There should be a comma after "Margret" to properly set off the appositive clause "who is always late for class." Correct.

**2.** Fernando was hired to work in one of the best law firms in New York City? No error
- A. "was hired" (underlined)
- B. "of the best law firms" (underlined)
- C. "City?" (underlined)

> Note: The sentence is a statement, not a question, so the question mark should be a period. C is correct.

> Note: The period after "tonight" should be a comma inside the quotation marks, since the sentence continues with the speech attribution. B targets the area of the error. Correct.

### Questions 9–15: Best Way to Write the Sentence

**Directions:** For questions 9–15, choose the best way to write the sentence.

**9.**
- A. With the help, of a map, and a compass, Cindy was able to find her way out of the forest.
- B. With the help of a map and a compass, Cindy was able to find her way out of the forest. (circled)
- C. With the help of a map and a compass Cindy was able to find her way out of the forest.
- D. With the help of a map and a. compass, Cindy was able to find her way, out of the forest.

> Student answer: B ✓ Correct.

**10.**
- A. "No!" I shouted repeatedly as rain began to fall on my head.
- B. "No!" I shouted, repeatedly as rain began to fall on my head. (struck through)
- C. "No!" I shouted repeatedly as rain began to fall on my head. (circled)
- D. "No" I shouted repeatedly as rain began to fall on my head. (struck through)

> Student answer: C
> Note: A and C appear identical in the OCR. Looking at the original, A ends with a period and C ends without one (or vice versa). The student circled C. Based on the scan, C appears to end with a period, making it correct.

**11.**
- A. Isabels' pet dog Spike likes to dig holes in the backyard. (struck through)
- B. Isabel's pet dog Spike, likes to dig holes in the backyard.
- C. Isabels pet dog, Spike, likes to dig holes, in the backyard. (struck through)
- D. Isabel's pet dog, Spike, likes to dig holes in the backyard. (circled)

> Student answer: D ✓
> Note: Needs possessive apostrophe on "Isabel's" and commas around the appositive "Spike." D has both. Correct.

**12.**
- A. Where does Greg's family live? (circled)
- B. Where does Gregs family live?
- C. Where does Greg's family live.
- D. Where does Gregs family live.

> Student answer: A ✓
> Note: Needs apostrophe in "Greg's" and a question mark. Only A has both. Correct.

**13.**
- A. Theresa drinks a lot of water; she is trying to cut down on sugary drinks. (circled)
- B. Theresa drinks a lot of water, she is trying to cut down on sugary drinks.
- C. Theresa drinks a lot of water she is trying to cut down on sugary drinks.
- D. Theresa, drinks a lot of water, she is trying to cut down on sugary drinks.

> Student answer: A
> Note: Student circled A but also appeared to circle B initially. A uses a semicolon to join two independent clauses, which is correct. B is a comma splice. A is correct.

**14.**
- A. Julius doesnt want to go to the beach because it is too hot outside.
- B. Julius doesn't want to go to the beach because it is too hot outside. (circled)
- C. Julius doesnt want to go to the beach, because it is too hot outside.
- D. Julius doesn't want to go to the beach; because it is too hot outside.

**15.**
- A. A volcanoes hot lava is very dangerous to touch. (struck through)
- B. A volcanoes' hot lava is very dangerous to touch.
- C. A volcanos hot lava is very dangerous to touch. (struck through)
- D. A volcano's hot lava is very dangerous to touch. (circled)
